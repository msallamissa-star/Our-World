# Credits

All media is real footage, photos, and recordings (no AI-generated, stylized, or cartoon content), with one parent-approved exception: the dinosaurs, which are extinct and so use real film of life size animatronic museum models (explained below). Each asset was checked visually before it was included, by reading a contact sheet of the poster, thumbnail, and gallery photos for every animal. Sounds were trimmed and volume-normalized; hero videos were trimmed to about 7 to 26 seconds and re-encoded to 720p H.264; photos were resized and screened to drop black-and-white or archival images. Full author details for each file live on its source page.

The app now has 32 animals: dog, cat, cow, sheep, pig, horse, donkey, alpaca, duck, chicken, bird, parakeet, macaw, peacock, lion, tiger, elephant, monkey, bear, zebra, panda, snake, frog, alligator, crocodile, an Ocean group (fish, dolphin, shark, turtle, jellyfish), and two dinosaurs (T-Rex and raptor). Hero clips now run up to about 40 seconds.

## Sources by type

- **Hero videos:** Wikimedia Commons (Creative Commons or public domain) for most animals, plus a few clean clips from Pixabay Videos (Pixabay Content License) kept from the first build. For 9 animals (dog, cat, sheep, pig, chicken, monkey, bird, frog, donkey) the hero is real footage of the animal visibly making its sound, with the original audio kept. For the others the hero is a clean real clip played muted while an accurate recorded sound plays in sync, because no licensed clip of that species vocalizing on camera was available. Every clip was visually verified to be real footage of the correct species. The bear hero is a clear wild brown bear fishing for salmon in Alaska; the cow's earlier Highland clip was replaced with a common Holstein; the panda hero was replaced with a clear HD clip of a giant panda eating bamboo in the snow (the earlier clip was blurry).
- **Recorded sounds:** Wikimedia Commons (Creative Commons or public domain). Examples: the cow moo is "Mudchute cow", the lion roar is "Lionroar.wav", the donkey bray is a Felix Blume field recording, the alligator hiss is "Alligatorhiss.ogg", the bear roar is the official Yellowstone National Park sound library grizzly roar (public domain), the monkey sound button is a real vervet monkey call (Chlorocebus pygerythrus) from a wildlife sound library, the parakeet is a real "Budgerigar chirping" recording, and the macaw and the peacock use real Xeno-canto and Commons bird calls. Loud calls (bear, lion, the two dinosaurs, macaw, peacock) are played a little quieter with a short fade-in so they do not startle a child held close to the device. Honest gaps: no isolated giant-panda call or crocodile hiss exists on Commons, so the panda and crocodile sound buttons use real scene audio from their own footage rather than an isolated call; and no real alpaca hum recording was available, so the alpaca has no sound file and its button instead speaks the animal's name (it is never faked).
- **Birds (new):** parakeet, macaw, and peacock are all real footage and real calls from Wikimedia Commons. The parakeet hero is wild green parakeets feeding, played muted with a real budgie chirp in sync; the macaw is a real scarlet macaw on a branch with a real macaw call; the peacock is a real peacock fanning its tail with a real peacock call.
- **Dinosaurs (parent-approved exception):** dinosaurs are extinct, so there is no real living footage or sound. With the parent's explicit choice, the two dinosaurs use real film of life size moving animatronic models at the Natural History Museum, London (Creative Commons), framed honestly in the app as animals from long ago. The roar for each is taken from the model's own audio in the clip. The gallery photos are real photographs of life size dinosaur models and animatronics from Wikimedia Commons. No copyrighted movie footage (for example Jurassic Park) is used anywhere, so the app stays clean to share or sell.
- **Gallery photos:** Wikimedia Commons (Creative Commons or public domain), plus the original dog and cow photos from Pixabay (Pixabay Content License). Each gallery photo was picked by sight from a numbered candidate sheet to keep clear, colour, real, toddler-appropriate images (for example the parakeet sheet's Ubuntu Budgie OS screenshot and the raptor sheet's gory carcass shot were both rejected by eye).
- **Grid thumbnails:** built from the first gallery photo of each animal (Creative Commons / public domain).

## New and re-sourced in this build (Wikimedia Commons, CC / public domain)

All checked by sight on a contact sheet before inclusion. Full per-file author and license details live on each Commons source page (compile them before any sale).

- **Tiger:** hero "Tiger playing with a piece of wood in a pool"; gallery photos are real tiger photographs (Bengal/Amur). The only real tiger recording on Commons was an aggressive snarl, which clashes with the calm framing, so no sound file ships and the button speaks the animal's name instead (never faked).
- **Snake:** hero "Elaphe climacophora - Japanese rat snake"; gallery "Indo-Chinese rat snake" set by Tisha Mukherjee. No honest snake recording on Commons, so the button speaks the name.
- **Fish (clownfish):** hero "Clownfish in anemone off Vanuatu"; gallery are Amphiprion ocellaris (orange clownfish) photos. Silent animal; the button speaks the name.
- **Dolphin:** hero "Bottlenose dolphin - kanagawa - 2025" (muted) + a real Felix Blume field recording of dolphin vocalizations, "161691 felixblume dolphin-screaming-underwater-in-caribbean-sea-mexico.wav" (trimmed, normalized, and played at the quieter LOUD volume with a fade-in so it stays gentle); gallery are real bottlenose dolphin photos.
- **Shark (whale shark):** hero "Swimming with a Whale Shark"; gallery are real whale-shark photographs. Silent animal; the button speaks the name.
- **Turtle:** hero "Green sea turtles (Chelonia mydas) in Hawaii"; gallery are real green sea turtle photos. Silent animal; the button speaks the name.
- **Jellyfish:** hero "Jellyfish at Monterey Bay Aquarium"; gallery are real moon-jellyfish photos. Silent animal; the button speaks the name.
- **Re-sourced videos (kept their existing real sounds):** lion "Ishikawa Zoo roaring lion" (Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 4.0), now visibly roaring in sync with the clean Lionroar.wav; bear "Brown bear fishing for salmon, Crescent Lake, Alaska"; horse "A grazing horse at the Écomusée de la Bintinais"; alpaca "Alpaca (Vicugna pacos)"; peacock "Male peacock tail display at Sofia Zoo". All muted, with their real sound (or none, for the alpaca) playing in sync.
- **Re-sourced ocean videos (silent, as these animals make no vocal sound):** jellyfish "Jellyfish at the Aquarium of Genoa" (Wikimedia Commons, CC BY-SA 4.0), clear drifting jellyfish; shark "Tiger shark (Galeocerdo cuvier) in Fiji" (Wikimedia Commons, CC BY 3.0), a clean open-water shark with no diver in frame.
